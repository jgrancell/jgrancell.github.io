Sanderson's Cosmere - My Suggested Reading Order

The Cosmere

By and large, if you read the Cosmere in published order you are safe. That being said, there are connections between the books that can be better understood if you read them in a specific order.

Books published before The Lost Metal are more or less standalone, however books published after The Lost Metal (excluding Tress and Yumi) are more interlinked and your comprehension will benefit from reading previous books first.

The reading order suggested below is my own, based on my own readings of the Cosmere and discussion with others.

Suggested Reading Order

  • Warbreaker and Mistborn Era 1
    • Warbreaker is a great standalone book if you want to get your feet wet, without committing to 3 books to start.
    • Mistborn Era 1 is the first 3 large books Sanderson published, and while compelling are some of his rougher works since they're earlier..
    • You will want to read all both Mistborn Era 1 and Warbreaker before moving on.
  • Elantris
  • In Any Order, But Completing All:
    • Mistborn Era 2, Books 1 through 3 only.
    • Stormlight Archives (including novella Edgedancer between Books 2 and 3, and Dawnshard between Books 3 and 4).
    • Arcanum Unbound Collection (excluding Mistborn: A Secret History).
  • Mistborn: A Secret History
    • This book contains spoilers for Mistborn Era 2 Book 3. Don't read it until you are done Era 2 Book 3.
    • This novella is part of the Arcanum Unbound Collection.
  • Mistborn Era 2, Book 4
  • All 3 Cosmere Secrect Novels:
    • Tress and the Emerald Sea is standalone and can be ready anywhere, but benefits from knowing Hoid beforehand.
    • Yumi and the Nightmare Painter is standalone and can be ready anywhere, but benefits from knowing Hoid beforehand.
    • The Sunlit Man has loose connections to a previous book, and should be read after completing the rest of the Cosmere listed above.
